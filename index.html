Neighborhood vision

A neighborhood vision guided by noble principles

Reduce cut-through traffic in the Rockridge trapezoid(*) by 1,000,000 cars / year

Guiding principles

  • Preserve and improve neighborly character
  • Enhance residents’ safety
  • Foster walkability and bike-friendliness

(*) Residential area delimited by 4 avenues — College, Claremont, Telegraph and Alcatraz — with 14 points of entry/exit for car traffic

Local traffic volume is breaching local and federal rules

Local ADT has increased steadily and will increase at a faster clip in the near future

Average Daily Traffic (ADT) on Colby Street

  • The adoption of navigation apps nationally is subverting the foundational concept of street and traffic stratification (Local, Collector, Arterial) sending cars and trucks into residential areas and streets designed for low traffic
  • The entire trapezoid is affected, especially Colby, Hillegass, and 62nd, which in turn feed traffic on all streets
  • Current volume is already breaching or about to breach local and federal rules:
    • Colby bike route with ADT ~3,900 vs. 2,000 ceiling2
    • Hillegass with ADT ~1,900 vs. 700 ceiling3
    • Martin with ADT >1,500 vs. 700 ceiling3
  • Learning from the Slow Neighborhood experiment during the COVID Shelter-in-Place, over 350 Rockridge Trapezoid residents petitioned the City to reduce traffic volume
(1) Your Navigation App Is Making Traffic Unmanageable | Institute of Transportation Studies
(2) OakDOT threshold for bike routes with ceiling of “2,000 ADT” and “50 vehicles per hour per direction at peak hour”
(3) US Department of Transportation / Federal Highway Administration p.24
(4) Environmental Impact Assessment as part of Jewish Community Center permitting
